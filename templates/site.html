What Are Deepfakes?

Deepfakes are synthetic media where a person's likeness (face, voice, etc.) is replaced with someone else's using artificial intelligence. The term "deepfake" combines "deep learning" and "fake."

These AI-generated forgeries can be incredibly convincing, making it difficult to distinguish between real and manipulated content. While the technology has legitimate uses in entertainment and education, it's increasingly being used for malicious purposes like misinformation, fraud, and harassment.

How Deepfakes Are Created

  • Training AI models on large datasets of images/videos of the target person
  • Using neural networks to learn facial features and movements
  • Swapping faces or altering expressions in existing media
  • Generating synthetic voice audio from text input

Common Uses of Deepfakes

  • Entertainment (e.g., de-aging actors, bringing historical figures to life)
  • Misinformation and fake news
  • Impersonation for fraud or blackmail
  • Non-consensual pornography ("revenge porn")

How to Spot Deepfakes

Visual Clues

Look for unnatural blinking, inconsistent lighting, blurring around edges, or facial distortions.

Audio Clues

Listen for unnatural speech patterns, robotic tones, or mismatched lip movements.

Video Quality

Deepfakes often have lower resolution than the original video, especially around the face.

Behavioral Cues

Watch for unnatural facial expressions or movements that don't match the person's typical behavior.
